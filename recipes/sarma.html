Sarma

Description

Serbian stuffed cabbage, or sarma, is a hearty dish that many Serbs serve every day but especially for special occasions like slavas and other holidays. Recipes vary from cook to cook, but they all consist of a meat filling wrapped in cabbage and cooked over sauerkraut.

Ingredients

  • 1 (3-to 4-pound) head cabbage
  • 1 pound ground chuck
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1 cup raw rice, rinsed
  • 1 (1.4-ounce) package dehydrated onion soup mix
  • 1 (32-ounce) jar sauerkraut, rinsed and drained
  • 6 medium smoked ribs, ham hocks, or other smoked meat

Steps

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Cut the core out of the cabbage and discard.
  3. In a large pot partially filled with boiling water, steam the cabbage, cut-side down. Reduce the heat to medium, cover and steam until the outer leaves are limp, about 8 to 10 minutes.
  4. Use tongs to remove the larger softened leaves. Continue to steam and remove larger leaves as they become done until you have about 22 to 23 whole large cabbage leaves.
  5. With a paring knife, remove the tough ribs without damaging the leaves. Discard the cabbage ribs or reserve for another use.
  6. In a large bowl, mix together the ground chuck, ground pork, rice, and onion soup mix.
  7. Add a small amount of water to make the mixture easier to handle.
  8. Set 3 or 4 of the leaves aside. Heap about 1/4 cup of filling onto each of the remaining cabbage leaves.
  9. Fold the bottom of the cabbage leaf up over the meat.
  10. Fold sides to the center and roll away from yourself to completely encase the meat.
  11. Repeat until all the meat filling is used.
  12. Position a rack in the center of the oven and heat to 350 F.
  13. Coarsely chop the remaining steamed cabbage, not including the reserved leaves.
  14. Spread the chopped cabbage on the bottom of a large casserole dish or Dutch oven. Add the drained sauerkraut.
  15. Layer on the cabbage rolls, seam-side down.
  16. Cut the smoked ribs into pieces. Space the ribs or other smoked meat of choice between the cabbage rolls.
  17. Cover rolls with reserved whole cabbage leaves.
  18. Cover the casserole dish and bake 1 hour.
  19. Reduce temperature to 325 F and bake for 2 more hours.
  20. Let sit 20 to 30 minutes before serving.
  21. Serve with the ribs.
